Mate Choice in Convict Cichlids

In many monogamous species, sexual selection takes place by mate choice, which is usually based on male characteristics. However, the presence of a breeding site also might be equally or more important than male size for substrate brooders. This study investigated the importance of a large mate versus the presence of a breeding site in the monogamous convict cichlid (Amatitlania siquia). If a breeding site is more important than male size, then we predict that females will choose the male with breeding site regardless of his size. To test our hypothesis, males were confined to separate compartments with only one male receiving a breeding site, but females swam freely. In the experimental groups, one male was larger than the other, but both males were equal in size for the control group. Females significantly chose small males with a breeding site more often than large males without a breeding site; therefore, we speculate that the presence of a breeding site is more important than size in convict cichlids.

I was responsible for contributing to creating and testing hypothese, as well as performing statistical tests on data and writing manuscript.

The Role Of Hydrodynamic Movement In Evoking Territorial Displays In The Dusky Farmerfish (Stegastes Nigrican) In An Arabian Sea Atoll

Many animals in nature vigorously defend areas called territories that contain resources like food, shelter and breeding sites. The herbivorous damselfish (Stegastes nigracans) typically inhabits coral reef and coral-rubble field territories where they farm algae and display stereotypic territorial behaviors (threat displays, chasing, etc.) in response to intruders. By presenting various visual cues such asshape, colors and/or movements, intruders induce these behaviors. During January of 2013 in a shallow lagoon off of the coast of Kadmat Island in the Laccadive Archipelago in the Arabian Sea (India), we presented three-dimensional models to territory-holding adult S. nigracans and captured on video resulting agnostic and non-agnostic displays. Compared to experimental controls which consisted of territory holders that were video recorded in absence of a model intruder, fish that were challenged with intruder models exhibited more stereotypic agnostic behaviors. We further evaluated the role of hydrodynamic movement in relation to a model‘s ability to evoke a behavior. Agnostic behaviors were grouped into different sub-groups so we could identify different factors. Aggressive behaviors showed a strong correlation with the degree of movement of the model. Other agnostic behaviors declined or showed no change with model movement. This study clearly indicates that intruder movement, in addition to (static)visual cues contributes to the overall degree of threat posed by intruding fish.

I was responsible for creating and testing developed hypothese, as well as performing statistical tests on data and writing manuscript.

Storm event effect on land use changes from hurricane Sandy on Long Island, NY

In 2012, Hurricane Sandy hit New York impacting home and business owners. Many lost property and personal posesstions from trees falling on personal object. In this study, we investigated if there has been in increase of tree biomass removal using newly developed techniques through timelapsed satelite (Google Maps) imagery.
